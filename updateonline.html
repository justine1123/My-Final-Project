Update

OCTOBER 9, 2020

Simon Aihiokhai, theology, presented “Rethinking Catholic Social Teachings in Light of Christian Identity in a Globalized World,” a talk given to members of the Faith and Formation Ambassador Program. Organized by the Moreau Center, University of Portland, October 5, 2020.
Elizabeth Lee, institutional research, accepted an invitation from the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities (NWCCU) to serve a one-year term on its Data Council. This representative team of higher education leaders will advise UP’s regional accreditation agency on advancing equity-centered transformation at NWCCU’s 163 member institutions using data-informed approaches. More information about the NWCCU is available at https://www.nwccu.org/.
Tammy VanDeGrift, Shiley School of Engineering, presented “Supporting and Teaching Students at Liberal Arts Colleges in Online Courses” at the Consortium for Computing Sciences in Colleges, Northwestern Conference (virtual but hosted by North Idaho College) on October 3. The panel featured colleagues from other Oregon institutions: Haiyan Cheng, Willamette University; Shereen Khoja, Pacific University; Anna Ritz; Reed College.
Gary Mitchell, business, wrote a textbook, (co-authored with Ted Klastorinm, UW) titled Project Management: A Risk Management Approach, published in September 2020 by SAGE Publications. The book is available in hardback and a variety of eBook formats including VitalSource and Kindle.
Jim Baillie, philosophy, was featured in the article “COVID-19 makes us think about our mortali,ty. Our brains aren’t designed for that,” in online edition of The Washington Post, October 3, and in the print edition on Tuesday, October 7. See the article using this link.
Jeffrey White, international languages & cultures, attended Supplemental Instruction (SI) QuickStart Training, International Center for Supplemental Instruction, University of Missouri-Kansas City, July 2020.
Corey Pressman, nursing had his poem, “All those ancient egyptians were right,” published in the “Works of Resistance, Resilience” issue of the About Place Journal. See the poem using this link.
